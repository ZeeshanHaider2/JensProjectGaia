Project Gaia

For more than fifty years the present writer has been deeply engaged in and concerned about the future of humankind in the light of industrial civilisation's extremely problematic relationship with the rest of the biosphere due to population growth, depletion of natural resources and harmful impact on the environment: not only global warming - much talked about, but next to nothing done with - , but also degra.dation of ecosystems, loss of biodiversity, extinction of species, extensive erosion of agricultural soil, worldwide pollution with toxic chemicals and plastics etc. Trying to influence public opinion in Denmark I took some years ago the initiative for and partly planned two international conferences in the Danish Parliament. The first one was about peak oil and the replacement of fossil fuels with renewable energy with the British oil geologist Colin Campbell as the main speaker who one and a half year later founded ASPO = Association for the Stdy of Peak Oil abd Gas. The second one originally was entitled "Is the World Moving Towards or Away From Ecological Sustainability?" and had the subtitle: "And if away what can we do about it?" In parallel with my many years of studies and thinking about the above-mentioned problems I have built up a substantial private library not only covering ecology and human ecology as well as the other natural and technical sciences, but also the social sciences and humanities. Topics of special interest has been ideas as drivers in human history, the two revolutions in communications in human history: writing and the printing press and their consequences, the Era of Enlightenment in the 17. and 18. centuries, the Industrial Revolution in Great Britain (1760-1820) and its ensuing spread to the rest of the world, and the Second World War and its origins. My reading has led me to some very different conclusions concerning how to diminish the risk of an ecological megacatastrophe and a collapse of industrial civilisation we are heading towards with lightening speed.
